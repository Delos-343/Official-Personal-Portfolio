1.   The Pressing Thing About Careers

Everyone's worried for about their future and the circumstances surrounding it. Where should I go? What will I do? Where should I start? All these are lithe questions with large-scale, long-term influences on a student.

Even I, as a person with amiable amounts of work experiences, remains trapped in this constant loop of overthinking. This so-called quarter life crisis characterized by negative awareness of self, existentialism, toxic productivity, and periods of burnouts.

2.   Let's Talk About Careers

First of all, I'd like to talk about my current career, its development, and the steps and sacrifices that were needed to get me here.

I can tell you, it wasn't easy. At least getting past the first stage, of which also happens to be the simplest stage; getting to know one's self, strengths and weaknesses, as well as implementing them to your goals. In short, it really is nothing more than a big, fat "Just Do It!" scenario. And do note that the following list only showcases my active job positions.

3.   Coming to Terms with Myself

There is a reason why some people called me inhuman. And now that I look back at myself, and think more on it, I can see why. Admittedly, and should I ever be so truthful, I feel trapped; in a cage of my own psyche. The insecurities and seething hatred I had for myself from my controversial childhood experiences seem to be the primary 'push' into doing this; into being this successful.

Years and years of strict discipline, both mentally and physically; forceful self-assessments and applied situational awareness to build confidence and maintain control; trust containment and objective humane treatment of other entities; treating fear as a punching bag, driven by hatred and rage unseen in any other person I've come across.

These are all the things that drives me forward. Whether pass the edge or simply into another corner, I don't know. But it'd worked so far and I expect it to do so for the time being. In fact, it's actually really fun.

  —   John Gretton “Jocko” Willink
