My Education

Welcome to the My Education section, visitor!

Here, you will find an overview of my educational history, extracurricular experiences, and other student activities related (or unrelated) to my field of study. Expect to read about my experiences in volunteering, final projects, startup competitions, as well as student clubs. Please note that the contents of this page will be inputted in descending order.

So, I wish you a great 20 minutes read ahead, visitor!

Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember

Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS) is a leading university in the field of science and technology in Indonesia. Established in 1960 in Surabaya, Indonesia, ITS aims to become a world-class university contributing to the independence of the nation and becoming a reference in education, research and community service, as well as the development of innovation, especially those that support the industry and the maritime sector.

Accepted in September 2020 as an international S1 student of Informatics (IUP), this place serves as a personal new start. A freshman only by title, not by experience, this was a grand opportunity for me to rebuild from the ground up via an alternate learning path. The more I explore on the facilities that this institute has to offer, the more paths I see for me to tread upon, and the more exceptional people that I may meet. The show isn't over yet, it seems. Alternatively, I think it's just beginning.

I will be very honest with you, despite the rapid and continued development of self, knowledge, and experience, I consider myself as an average student. Nothing special nor notable in terms of achievements. In fact, I have zero luck in student organizations; on the contrary, I have seen significant success in professional fields such as open job positions and campaign volunteer candidacy.

  • 1
    Academics


    The courses I took as an Informatics student required a basic understanding of programming skills, arithmetics, and human-computer logic. From Basic Programming with C and C++ to OOP with Java and DBS with MySQL; the opportunities are endless.

  • 2
    Unit Kegiatan Mahasiswa


    Passionate to be an Agent of Change, I joined ITS Model United Nations for a year, serving as the Delegate of Germany in the ITS x UNDIP Joint Simulation, and of Russia in the annually-held MUN General Assembly.

  • 3
    Volunteering


    I was invited by a colleague to attend TEDX ITS as a volunteer guest in their social experiment projects, where I am able to interact and form a safe space with new people, and engage in open conversations.



  • 4
    Technopreneurship


    I was recruited by a startup team lead by ITS students bearing an interesting concept and realistic end goals. That project eventually became IlmuDewantara, a platform of professionalism, networking, and career development for students. I was appointed as one of its co-founders and executives as Chief Technology Officer (CTO).

  • 5
    Competitions


    Before the development of IlmuDewantara, I was the type of student that remained uninterested in joining competitions. Now, I feel empowered to actively pursue any competitions that they had joined, achieving 1st Runner's Up for Java Business Case Competition (JBC) and the Top 25 Finalists for the R'nB Competition and Mentorship Selection.

  • 6
    Student Honors


    Lastly, I had also earned the trust and admiration of the Department of Informatics ITS' administration, and was given the honors of being invited to special one-of-a-kind events and meetings held by the administration. For example, I was chosen to present in the ASIIN Site Visit 2021 as a stakeholder.

Universiti Putra Malaysia

Universiti Putra Malaysia (UPM) is an international, public university that has roots dating back to 1931, when it was first established as the Malaysian School of Agriculture. Despite a series of name changes over the years, it has maintained a focus on agriculture while also becoming a prominent research university.

Accepted in October 2018 as an international student in the undergraduate's programme for Biomedical Sciences, this place was a beautiful, albeit flawed, piece of memory. A true freshman, this was a long and arduous journey in the next steps of my life. Still unprepared to lead a life of independence, this phase was defined by countless mistakes, literal plot twists, and constant self-assessments and development.

As for experience, I managed to study here for circa 2 years, participating in student organizations and extra credit classes unrelated to my field of study. On the contrary, I have achieved many a great things here as well; managing to complete my first specialst certifications trials and given the opportunity to apply for an acceleration course on military medicine.

    Volunteering


    I planned to join the student division of the International Red Cross as soon as I complete specialist training in my third year.
